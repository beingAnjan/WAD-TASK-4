About

CSE

CSE can stand for Computer Science Engineering or the Centre for Science and Environment. Computer Science Engineering is an academic discipline that combines computer science and electrical engineering to design, develop, and maintain computer software and hardware, covering topics like programming, algorithms, data structures, and networks.

AIML

AIML most commonly stands for Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning when referring to an academic or engineering field of study. It also refers to the Artificial Intelligence Markup Language, a specific language used for building chatbots.

IOT

The Internet of Things (IoT) is a network of physical objects embedded with sensors, software, and other technologies that allow them to collect and exchange data over the internet. This network includes everything from smart home appliances to sophisticated industrial machinery, enabling automation, data-driven insights, and new digital services. IoT systems work by using sensors to collect data, a connectivity method (like Wi-Fi or 5G) to transmit it, and cloud or edge processing to analyze the data and trigger actions, often through a user interface.

ML

Machine learning is a subfield of artificial intelligence that allows computer systems to learn from data and improve without being explicitly programmed for every task. It uses algorithms to identify patterns in large datasets, enabling systems to make predictions or decisions on new data. Common applications include image and speech recognition, recommendation systems, and fraud detection.

DS

Data science is the interdisciplinary field of study that uses scientific methods, algorithms, and programming to extract actionable insights and knowledge from data. It combines mathematics, statistics, computer science, and domain expertise to analyze large datasets, uncover hidden patterns, and build predictive models to solve real-world problems and guide strategic decision-making in organizations.
